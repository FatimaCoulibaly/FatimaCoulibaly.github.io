What is Bossa Nova?

Origins

Bossa Nova is a mix between American jazz and Brazilian samba. The origin of this sweet melody genre started in Rio de Janeiro in 1958 by the composer, Antonio Carlos Jobim, along with the poet and other composer Vinicius de Moraes, and the guitarist, Joao Gilberto. Bossa Nova derived from Samba and is a softer version of the genre. Unlike Samba, Bossa Nova didnt incorporate a huge amount of percussion into the songs. Drums and other percussions were still used in this genre, but they werent at the forefront. Instruments such as classical and acoustic guitars, pianos, the electric organ were used to create a calm and optimistic feeling for listeners.

Meanings

The poetic lyrics captivated people, especially the youth. Themes such as love, happiness, sadness, longing, celebration and other themes evoke a feeling of hopefulness into the Brazilian youth. Musicians of this genre were mostly non political in their songs. Due to the fact that many people enjoyed this new sound, the genre was called Bossa Nova, which translates to new trend. The term, Bossa, was slang that was often used in the 1930s. It meant to have a flair or to be original. The word, Nova, means new in Portuguese. This name fitted the musical style because Bossa Nova was a new way of playing samba.

Lasting Impact

From 1958-1963 Bossa Nova reached its greatest heights and achieve popularity globally. 1964 would be an end of Bossa Novas spotlight in Brazil. In a coup d'etat, the Brazilian Armed Forces overthrew President Joao Goulart. This new regime censored many Brazilian artists of various genres. The genre to come up next on the scene would be Musica Populara Brasileria (MPB).

Despite the governments censorship of musicians, Bossa Nova had left its stamp on the world. Its music will be continued to be enjoyed for decades to come. This unique blend of samba and jazz had the attention of audiences from all over the world. Bossa Nova became an internationally known genre and people would often associate it with Brazil.
